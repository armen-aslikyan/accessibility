Détails de l'Audit 7 Erreurs

Article RGAA 11.9 ID: button-name

Buttons must have discernible text

Ensure buttons have discernible text

Impact Business

If your 'Submit' button is just an icon with no text, it's a ghost button for 20% of users.

Solution Technique

Add text or an aria-label to all <button> and <input type='submit'> elements.

Article RGAA 3.2 ID: color-contrast

Elements must meet minimum color contrast ratio thresholds

Ensure the contrast between foreground and background colors meets WCAG 2 AA minimum contrast ratio thresholds

Impact Business

Directly impacts readability for everyone in sunlight or on low-quality screens. Fails the 'Inclusive Luxury' test.

Solution Technique

Darken the text color or lighten the background to hit the 4.5:1 ratio (3:1 for large text).

Article RGAA 9.1 ID: heading-order

Heading levels should only increase by one

Ensure the order of headings is semantically correct

Impact Business

SEO Damage: Google uses headings to understand your content. Skipping from H1 to H4 confuses the crawler.

Solution Technique

Ensure headings follow a logical order (H1 > H2 > H3). Do not skip levels for styling purposes.

Article RGAA 8.3 ID: html-has-lang

<html> element must have a lang attribute

Ensure every HTML document has a lang attribute

Impact Business

UX Disaster: Screen readers will read French text with an English accent (or vice versa).

Solution Technique

Add the lang attribute to the <html> tag (e.g., <html lang='fr'>).

Article RGAA 1.1 ID: image-alt

Images must have alternative text

Ensure <img> elements have alternative text or a role of none or presentation

Impact Business

Total exclusion of visually impaired users. High SEO penalty.

Solution Technique

Add an 'alt' attribute describing the information conveyed by the image.

Article RGAA 6.1 ID: link-name

Links must have discernible text

Ensure links have discernible text

Impact Business

Total failure for screen reader users. Hearing 'Learn More' 20 times in a row provides zero information.

Solution Technique

Change generic text to descriptive text (e.g., 'Learn more about our sustainability report') or add an 'aria-label'.

Article RGAA 9.3 ID: list

<ul> and <ol> must only directly contain <li>, <script> or <template> elements

Ensure that lists are structured correctly

Impact Business

Inconsistent UX: A screen reader won't announce 'List of 5 items' if you just use <div> tags with bullet icons.

Solution Technique

Use <ul> or <ol> for groups of related items, and <li> for the items themselves.
